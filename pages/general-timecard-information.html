Timecard Basics

Submit all time cards to Media Services via the Payroll Coordinator.

Please contact Operations Manager to obtain blank time cards. Additional time card paperwork such as I9’s and Wage theft forms can be found on the project drive in the payroll folder.

When submitting timecards, make sure that the following information is included in the documentation:

Production Company Name, Job Name, Job Number

Union and Occupation Description

Employee Name and SS# (required for loan-out employees as well)

Employee address

Loan-out Name and Federal ID if applicable

Check off indication Male/Female

Hourly Rate in Rate field (ensuring it is same for all days per timecard)

Work Dates

All work and meal in-and-out times

Employee and production signatures

Completed Start / Close Form on the back of each timecard

Completed W-4, I-9 & Wage Theft Protection Act Form

Wage Theft Protection Act Form must be prefilled with hourly rates

No Altered Timecards

Altering timecards in any way - including in-and-out times without the initialed approval of the employee is unacceptable and timecards will be returned. Please Email or fax a timecard that needs to be changed to the employee and have them make all changes, or call the employee to verify that alterations to the timecard are approved prior to making changes.

Minimum Hourly Wage

Be certain that employees are paid at least minimum wage (for both union and non-union) for their appropriate occupation, especially if a daily guarantee is used. The word "flat" must not appear anywhere on the timecard. Lord Danger does not pay "premium overtime." For example, if a crew member works on a 10-hour guarantee, then overtime is based on dividing the guarantee by 11, not 10. If on a 12-hour guarantee, then overtime is based on dividing the guarantee by 14, not 12. Also, Media Services Universal will not pay a timecard with multiple rates. Have the employee fill out multiple timecards or average the rates so that the gross and hours match.

Union Timecards

Crew members working union occupations must complete the "Union Box" on the timecard with their correct union local number, regardless of their union affiliation unless it is an Internet production or a Television, Film, or Tape production. Lord Danger is a signatory to the multi-state agreement and is legally obligated to pay union benefits.

Crew Rates

Crew rates should not exceed the budgeted amounts. If other rates are requested, please discuss with the Production Manager. Without the approval of the PM, Lord Danger will not pay crew rates above those provided for in the budget.

Non-Union Crew & Talent

All must complete a Wage Theft Prevention Act Form. These can be found in the “Contracts and Releases” folder in the Payroll Folder on Google Drive.

IRS Tax Forms

I-9‘s & W-4‘s must be filled out at the beginning of each new job. The W-4 and I-9s can be found on the back of and attached to the timecards. Indicating that an I-9 or W-4 is "on file" will only be accepted and processed after having been properly completed at the end of each job.

One Week per Timecard

A workweek runs from Sunday to Saturday. If an employee works only a couple of days one week and a couple of days the next week, then he/she should fill out two timecards.

Mileage or Kit/Box Rentals

Enter this information in the appropriate section of the timecard including a line number. Do not include these amounts in the gross pay box. Attach a copy of the mileage or kit/box rental form along with the timecard in order to receive payment.

Meals

Meals must be reflected on the timecard. Under California Law, all non-exempt employees are entitled to a meal break every 6 hours. A meal penalty for a non-union employee is equal to one-hour base pay per day. There are no multiple meal penalties for non-union employees. Direct any questions regarding meal penalties for union or non-union employees to the PM.

No Cash Labor Payments

We do not pay cash for labor. All payments for labor are to be made through our payroll service, or invoice.

• Filled out timecard with loan-out name & number, or invoice

• Current W9

• I9

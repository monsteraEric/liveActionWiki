General Information (Covid)

General Covid Protocols

Beginning in August of 2021, all on-set personnel must verify they are fully vaccinated at least 2 weeks prior to being on set. All must provide, either digitally or physically, their vaccination card to the Covid Compliance Officer before entering the set.

Per the CDC, “fully vaccinated” means receiving both (2) doses of the Pfizer and Moderna vaccines or (1) a single dose of the Johnson and Johnson vaccine.

Per the CDC, it takes 2 weeks for a fully vaccinated person to build immunity to COVID-19.

More information can be found here: https://www.cdc.gov/coronavirus/2019-ncov/vaccines/different-vaccines/how-t hey-work.html

No one with any symptoms of COVID-19 is allowed on set.

No one will be disciplined for reporting any issues related to COVID 19 on set.

If anyone starts feeling sick on set, they will be sent home for the day, with pay.

All sets are closed. NO visitors OR in and outs, for the crew, talent, clients, etc.

NO physical contact of any kind – no handshakes, high fives, elbow bumps, hugs, etc.

Everyone must stay 6’ or more apart on set.

Do not touch your face – nose, mouth, eyes, etc.

All talent and crew will be required to have temperature checks daily and randomly throughout the day. No discrimination in these checks will be tolerated.

All crew must fill out & sign a daily symptoms verification list before entering the set. Getting tested for COVID is required for anyone attending our shoot.

Talent and crew tests should be conducted within 48 hours of the shoot. A negative result is required and results must be provided.

Rapid tests are conducted on the day of the shoot prior to walking on set for added safety.

COVID 19 Compliance Officer (HSS or Health Safety Supervisor)

All sets must have a COVID 19 certified specialty officer (HSS, or Health Safety Supervisor) to manage and monitor COVID protocol and management.

They should be given PA’s as support for cleaning and other duties.

They should be given PPE and sanitary supplies as needed.

Training & Daily Health Management

HSS or Medic must verify negative test results to allow entry to set. Temperature Checks are required (On entry & throughout the day) Daily Health Questionnaire is required.

Review all protocols below with all crew EVERY MORNING.

Ensure everyone understands:

How we currently believe COVID 19 spreads, to the best of our knowledge.

The signs/symptoms of COVID 19, to the best of our knowledge.

Fever

Cough

Shortness of breath or difficulty breathing

Chills

Repeated shaking with chills

Headache

Sore throat

New loss of taste or smell
